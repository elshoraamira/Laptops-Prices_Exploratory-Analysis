Effect of Laptop Components on Their Prices¶

by Amira ElShora¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the components of laptops that could be used to predict their prices. The main focus was on the main operation components: Processor Type (CPU), RAM Size (in GB) and GPU Brand.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted of prices and attributes of approximately 1300 laptops. The attributes included my predictors, as well as additional features such as type, size, and weight. Sixty-four data points were removed from the analysis due to inconsistencies or duplicate information.

Distribution of Laptops Prices¶

Laptops prices in the dataset take on a very large range of values, from about 170 at the lowest to about 6100 at the highest. Plotted on a logarithmic scale, the distribution of diamond prices takes on a unimodal shape.

Price VS. CPU¶

Most expensive laptops are those with Inter Core processor
Laptops with other processor types are almost equal in price

Price VS. RAM¶

Most expensive laptops are those with 64GB RAM
Laptops with 8GB are the most common as well as with average price
Laptops with 2GB are the cheapest of them all

Price VS. CPU, RAM, GPU & Type¶

CPU, GPU & Type record a price range of hundreds to 2000, On the other hand the RAM curve is rising dramatically to price ranges up to 4000, For that we can expect that RAM is a very influential factor in setting price for a laptop. (The larger the RAM size the more expensive the laptop is)

Price by RAM & CPU¶

The most expensive Laptop is one with CPU of Type Intel Core and 64GB RAM
The cheapest laptops are those with CPU of the other types with RAM size of 2 - 8 GB

Price by RAM & CPU for GPU Brands¶

Nivida GPU comes with Intel processor almost in all cases
The most expensive Laptop is one with CPU of Type Inel Core, 64GB RAM and GPU Brand Nividia
The cheapest laptops are those with CPU of the other types with RAM size of 2 - 8 GB and GPU Brand AMD
